1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device when you visit. They are widely used to make websites function properly, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to the site owner about how the site is being used.

This policy covers cookies and similar tracking technologies including web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage objects. Cookies are set either by the website you are visiting (first-party cookies) or by third-party services such as CRM tools (third-party cookies). Some cookies are deleted when you close your browser (session cookies). Others remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them (persistent cookies).


2. How OnChainMake Uses Cookies

Validation Phase (Phase 0 and 0.1): We use cookies to operate the website, manage your session if you create a test account, and understand how visitors interact with the site so we can improve it before commercial launch. HubSpot tracking cookies are active from the moment the website is live.

Commercial Phase (Phase 0.2 onwards): Additional functionality cookies will be required to support the platform, including maintaining your logged-in session, remembering your wallet connection preferences, and supporting the escrow workflow. This policy will be updated before any new cookies are introduced.

4. Your Cookie Choices


4.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you visit onchainmake.com for the first time, a cookie consent banner will appear. You can accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or manage preferences individually. Your choice is stored in the ocm_cookie_consent cookie for 12 months. You can change your preference at any time using the cookie settings link in the website footer.


4.2 What Happens If You Reject Non-Essential Cookies

The website will still function. The public website and survey will work normally. If you create a platform account, session and security cookies will still be set as they are strictly necessary. HubSpot will not track your visit. We will not be able to measure how visitors find and use the site.
